Overview

BIOTIFY is an app that brings the beauty of nature to the palm of your hands. Centred on the gorgeous landscapes of Vancouver, B.C., this app allows users to collect and identify various plants, animals, and significant landmarks throughout the area.

I created this design as part of a collaborative student project which aimed to become an educational tool for Vancouver residents, new and old, to learn more about the natural and cultural history of the land they live on.

Our Audience

For this project, my team and I chose to try and solve challenges users face when it came to outdoor exploration in Vancouver. As this area is known for having quite beautiful scenery, there are a wide variety of outdoor activities that are popular: from hiking to biking to simply going on leisurely walks around the waterfront.

With so many options, even we were getting overwhelmed with the amount of advice, information, tips, and more that were available online. This realization made us focus our efforts into making an app that could help people with similar confusion and frustrations. We decided to specifically aim for users who were new or relatively uninformed about Vancouver’s scenery.

Finding the Problem

To gain a better understanding, I conducted secondary research to figure out what some of the more common challenges for newcomers were in the area. My research included:

  • reading through blog and forum posts on Vancouver-based social platforms ie: r/vancouverhiking)
  • gathering 2-3 personal anecdote stories from more experienced residents in the area
  • reading scientific research articles that examined the psychology behind motivation in intensive outdoor activities such as hiking or running researching reviews for existing exploration or fitness apps (ie: Strava for hiking or MerlinID for animal exploration)
